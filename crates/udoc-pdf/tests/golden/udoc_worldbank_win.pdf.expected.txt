 The project was to:(a) improve primary and secondary education quality, targeting opportunities for the poor, and providing better 
access in the atolls; (b) reduce the shortage of teachers; (c) upgrade atoll primary teachers; (d) focus on greater female 
participation; (e) reduce skill shortages; (f) upgrade staff at the health, vocational, management and teacher training institutions; (g) 
increase fellowship training opportunities by allocating training to both public and private sector recipients; (h) and strengthen 
financial management of the education sector.

public and private trainees in health, vocational and management education; (d) supported overseas post-secondary fellowship 
training for qualified Maldivian students; (e) assisted with management and policy support in the education sector; and (f) undertook 
priority studies in the education sector.

3. Achievement of Relevant Objectives:
Objectives were substantially achieved .  Secondary school access was increased, particularly outside the capital .  
The only objective not achieved involved financial management of the education sector .
4. Significant Outcomes/Impacts:
Though no specific targets were created at appraisal, many students and teachers benefited from the project . The 
objective of providing 1400 additional places for secondary students was surpassed, with  1708 students enrolling by 
the end of the project (53% female).  Students were able to go to secondary school in the atolls near their homes  
rather than Male, the capital; the proportion of student enrollment in Male declined from  80% to 55%.  Teacher 
training facilities were built and remodeled, and about  2440 atoll primary school teachers received inservice training;  
also 52 lower-secondary school teachers received training . The proportion of untrained primary-school teachers 
dropped form 40% to 10%. Student-teacher ratios were lowered from about 1:25 in Male to 1:17 in Male and 1:15 in 
the atolls.  The Maldives Centre of Management and Administration was built, and about  467 students received 
training in areas such as accounting, administration, secretarial skills, information technology .  About 651 fellowships 
were given for overseas post-secondary training. in various areas needed by the local economy .  Studies were 
carried out on public examinations issues and provision of post -secondary education; costs are partly recovered  
through tuition.  Communities are involved in maintaining the schools, and participate in their activities .
5. Significant Shortcomings (including non-compliance with safeguard policies):
The percentage of expatriate teachers  (75% at appraisal) at the secondary level remained constant, indicating a  
continuing need for more teaching personnel .  The financial management systems proposed during appraisal were  
not implemented, and a unit of managing statistical information was not established, partly due to the liquidation of a
